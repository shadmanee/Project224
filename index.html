About Bangladesh

  • Official Name: People's Republic of Bangladesh
  • Form of Government: Unitary Parliamentary Democracy
  • Capital: Dhaka
  • Population: 161,376,708
  • Demonym(s): Bangladeshi
  • Official Language(s): Bengali
  • State Religion: Islam
  • Currency: Bangladeshi Taka (BDT)
  • Area: 148,460 sq. km (57,320 sq. mi)
  • Boundaries: India, Myanmar, the Bay of Bengal
  • Time Zone: UTC+6 (BST)

A Historical Timeline of Bangladesh

Ancient and Classical

The period of Bengal's history ranging over the Stone Age, Iron Age and several dynasties.

Medieval and Early Modern

Emergence of Sultanates as Bengal sees an end to centuries of Jain, Hindu and Buddhist rules.

Mughal Period

The Mughal absorption of Bengal led by empreror Babur.

Hindu States

Hindu states established in and around Bengal during the early modern period.

Maratha Invasions

Early 18th century invasions led by the Maratha Empire.

Colonial Bengal

Europian colonial settlements in Bengal leading up to the British crown rule.

British Crown Rule

Fall of the Bengal Nawabs and Bengal under the British East India Company rule.

Partition of Bengal

The partition in 1947 caused by the breakdown of Hindu Muslim unity.

Post-partition Indian Bengal

The Hindu-dominated western part of British Bengal Province became the Indian state of West Bengal.

East Pakistan

The borders of modern Bangladesh were established, and the region became East Pakistan as a part of the newly formed State of Pakistan.

Liberation War

24 years of political and nationalist movements leading up to a nine-month-long war, resulting in the emergence of the People's Republic of Bangladesh.

Bangladesh

Emergence of Bangladesh as a sovereign state on 16th December, 1971.
