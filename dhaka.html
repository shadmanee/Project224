Dhaka
the economic, political, and cultural center of Bangladesh

Lalbagh Fort

Lalbagh Fort (also Fort Aurangabad) is an incomplete 17th-century Mughal fort complex. The construction was started in 1678 AD by Mughal Subahdar Muhammad Azam Shah, who was a son of Emperor Aurangzeb and later emperor himself. His successor, Shaista Khan, did not continue the work, though he stayed in Dhaka up to 1688.

Location

The fort stands before the Buriganga River in the southwestern part of Dhaka, Bangladesh.

Ahsan Manzil

Ahsan Manzil is the erstwhile official residential palace and seat of the Nawab of Dhaka. Construction was started in 1859 and was completed in 1872. It was constructed in the Indo-Saracenic Revival architecture. It has been designated as a national museum.

Location

The building is situated at Kumartoli along the banks of the Buriganga River in Dhaka, Bangladesh.

View Map

National Parliament Building

The National Parliament House (bn. জাতীয় সংসদ ভবন) is the house of the Parliament of Bangladesh. Designed while the country was still part of Pakistan by architect Louis Kahn, the complex is one of the largest legislative complexes in the world, comprising 200 acres (810,000 m2).

Location

The building is located at Sher-e-Bangla Nagar in the Bangladeshi capital of Dhaka.
